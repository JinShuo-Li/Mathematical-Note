Book structure

The current manuscript is organized into four thematic parts.

The structure begins with optional foundations, develops core undergraduate mathematics, then turns to spaces, complex structure, discrete models, and probability.

I

Optional Foundations

Logic, proof language, naive set theory, axiomatic set theory, and ZFC as the controlled foundation for later chapters.

II

Core Undergraduate Mathematics

Analysis I, linear algebra, abstract algebra, and Analysis II form the main technical spine of the notes.

III

Spaces and Complex Structure

Topology and complex analysis extend the same formal habits into spaces, continuity, analytic functions, and residues.

IV

Discrete and Probabilistic Models

Graph theory and probability close the route with relational, random, and measure-theoretic viewpoints.

Mathematics is presented here as a structured ascent: intuition becomes language, language becomes axiom, and axiom becomes reusable theory.

Map of the notes

Every chapter has a matching practice supplement.

The current build includes front matter, a reader's guide, Chapters 0 through 9, practice supplements after each chapter, and an afterword in both editions.

Front

Preface and reader's guide

Motivation, reading strategy, and the conceptual promise of an axiomatic approach.

Ch. 0

Fundamentals

Propositional logic, predicate logic, proof language, notation, and basic formal habits.

Ch. 1

The Axioms of ALL

Naive set theory, axiomatic set theory, ZFC, and foundational viewpoints.

Ch. 2

Mathematical Analysis I

Number systems, limits, real numbers, derivatives, integration, and improper integrals.

Ch. 3

Linear Algebra

Linear equations, matrices, determinants, vectors in Euclidean space, transformations, and abstract vector spaces.

Ch. 4

Abstract Algebra

Operations, algebraic structures, groups, rings, fields, Galois theory, and modules.

Ch. 5

Mathematical Analysis II

Series, Euclidean limits and continuity, multivariable differential calculus, and multiple integrals.

Ch. 6

Topology

Metric spaces, topological spaces, bases, closed sets, connectedness, and compactness.

Ch. 7

Complex Analysis

Complex differentiability, Cauchy-Riemann equations, contour integration, power series, singularities, and residues.

Ch. 8

Graph Theory

Graph terminology, paths, circuits, trees, forests, and random graphs.

Ch. 9

Probability Theory

Classical probability, conditional probability, random variables, measure-theoretic foundations, limit theorems, and stochastic processes.
